Fusion 360 3D Printing Assembly

Cogsworth Clock.

A character CAD study translating a beloved animated clock into a printable 3D model — fabrication constraints drove every design decision, from clearance fits to split-line placement.

Year
2025
Role
CAD · DFM · Assembly
Duration
6 weeks
Output
Printable multi-part model
Overview

"The goal was a model that looks like Cogsworth from across the room and still prints cleanly on a hobby FDM machine — which meant every feature had to earn its place against overhangs, tolerances, and part count."

How it came together.

Process · 03 steps
01
Step 01 · Reference
Character study
Pulled screengrabs and film stills, mapped Cogsworth's proportions onto a clean silhouette sheet, and flagged the features that had to stay (cross-hatched face, ornate feet) vs. the ones I could simplify without losing the read.
02
Step 02 · CAD
Part breakdown
Built the body as a split shell to hide seams at parting lines. Designed the pendulum, arms, and base as separate components with 0.2mm clearance press fits so the model could be printed in pieces and snapped together without glue.
03
Step 03 · Print
Iterate & tune
Printed three prototypes tuning overhang angles, wall thicknesses, and fit tolerances. Moved from PLA to PETG for the moving parts once the kinematics were locked, and added small chamfers on every mating edge to make first-time assembly forgiving.
