SolidWorks DFM Ergonomics

Electric Screwdriver.

A teardown-to-redesign case study on a consumer power tool — profiling torque behavior, re-working the grip for smaller hands, and proposing DFM changes that cut part count without touching the feel.

Year
2024
Role
Teardown · CAD · DFM
Duration
8 weeks
Output
Redesign proposal & report
Overview

"Tore down an $18 consumer screwdriver, measured what it actually did versus what the box said it did, and used the gap as the brief for a redesign — fewer parts, better grip, same motor budget."

How it came together.

Process · 03 steps
01
Step 01 · Teardown
Measure what's there
Disassembled the unit down to the last fastener, logged every component with calipers and a gram scale, and ran a torque bench test to map real-world performance against the spec sheet.
02
Step 02 · Analysis
Find the gap
Flagged the grip's hot spots for smaller hands, identified six parts that could be consolidated, and traced torque-drop under load to a clutch spring that was specced for a different motor.
03
Step 03 · Redesign
Propose the fix
CAD'd a new shell around a re-located battery pack, wrote the DFM brief for the consolidated drive train, and ran tolerance stack-ups against the existing supplier drawings so the proposal could drop in without re-tooling.
